# Executive summary

"You must change your life" - borrowing from philosopher Peter Sloterdijk, this our annoying message for all publishers, writers, editors and designers working in the field of arts- and design-oriented publishing who want to transition towards electronic publishing or hybrid print and electronic publishing - if electronic publishing should not be an afterthought in your production chain and product portfolio, but become at least equally important as print.

In short, the transition will require a rethinking of traditional publication formats, a rethinking of editorial and production workflows, and last not least a rethinking of distribution. This will be even the case if your publication won't drastically change its form and content in the electronic medium. These publishers who wish to transition from pure print publishing to hybrid print and electronic publishing often experience a mismatch between their expectations and the reality of the new medium. Many publishers, editors, designers and artists overestimate the interactive and multimedia possibilities of electronic publishing - especially in relation to production budgets in small editions publishing. But conversely, they underestimate that electronic publishing questions many traditional publication formats such as: the anthology, the catalogue, the periodical. They also tend to underestimate the necessities of changing editorial processes and technical workflows.
